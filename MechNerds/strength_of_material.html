Strength Of Material


1.Stress (σ)

Stress is the internal force per unit area acting on a material. It is a measure of the intensity of internal forces within the material.
Stress Formula: σ= F/A
Where: σ = Stress (Pa or N/m²)
F = Force acting on the material (N)
A = Cross-sectional area of the material (m²)

2.Strain (ε)

Strain is the measure of deformation or elongation of a material due to stress.
Strain Formula: ε= L/ΔL
​ Where: ε = Strain (unitless)
ΔL = Change in length of the material
L = Original length of the material

3.Hooke's Law

Hooke's Law states that the stress is directly proportional to the strain within the elastic limit of a material.
Hooke's Law Formula: σ=E⋅ε
Where: E = Young's Modulus (Elastic Modulus) of the material (Pa or N/m²)

4. Young's Modulus (E)

Young's Modulus is a material property that measures the stiffness or elasticity of a material. It represents the ratio of stress to strain within the elastic region.
Young's Modulus Formula: E=ε/σ ​

5. Shear Stress (τ) and Shear Strain (γ)

Shear stress is the internal force per unit area acting parallel to the cross-sectional area of the material.
Shear strain is the change in shape or distortion of a material due to shear stress.
Shear Stress Formula: τ= F/A
Shear Strain Formula: γ= h/Δx ​
Where: τ = Shear Stress (Pa or N/m²)
F = Applied shear force (N)
A = Cross-sectional area of the material (m²)
Δx = Displacement parallel to the applied force
h = Original height of the material

6. Elastic Limit and Yield Strength

The elastic limit is the maximum stress a material can withstand without undergoing permanent deformation.
The yield strength is the stress at which the material starts to exhibit significant plastic deformation.
