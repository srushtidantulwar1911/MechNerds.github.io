Pumps and Compressor


Centrifugal Pumps

Centrifugal pumps are the most common type of pumps used for low-to-medium viscosity fluids. They use a rotating impeller to create a flow of fluid by converting rotational energy into kinetic energy.
The kinetic energy is then converted to pressure energy as the fluid exits the impeller.

Positive Displacement Pumps

Positive displacement pumps trap a fixed amount of fluid and displace it into the discharge pipe with each pump cycle. They provide a consistent flow rate regardless of changes in the discharge pressure.
Common types include reciprocating pumps (piston and diaphragm pumps) and rotary pumps (gear, screw, and vane pumps).

Diaphragm Pumps

Diaphragm pumps use a flexible diaphragm to displace fluid.
They are often used for metering applications, handling corrosive or abrasive fluids, and situations where leak-tight operation is critical.

Submersible Pumps

Submersible pumps are designed to be submerged in the fluid they are pumping, commonly used for dewatering, sewage pumping, and deep well water extraction.

Reciprocating Compressors

Reciprocating compressors use a piston-cylinder arrangement to compress the gas.
They are suitable for high-pressure applications and can handle varying flow rates.

Centrifugal Compressors

Centrifugal compressors use a high-speed impeller to accelerate gas and convert kinetic energy into pressure energy.
They are ideal for high-volume, low-pressure applications.

Rotary Compressors

Rotary compressors use rotating elements like screws, vanes, or lobes to compress gas.
They are more compact and operate smoothly with lower noise levels.
