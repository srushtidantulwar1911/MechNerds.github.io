Mechanical Instruments

In mechanical engineering, various measuring instruments are used to accurately assess and quantify physical quantities.
These instruments play a crucial role in manufacturing, research, and maintenance processes, ensuring precision, quality, and safety.


Types Of Measuring Instruments
  • CALLIPER
  • MICROMETER
  • DIAL INDICATOR
  • SURFACE ROUGHNESS TESTER
  • COORDINATE MEASURING MACHINE (CMM)
  • TACHOMETER
  • THERMOCOUPLES AND THERMOMETERS
  • PROTRACTORS AND ANGLE FINDERS
Calliper

A caliper is a versatile instrument used to measure the dimensions of an object with high accuracy. It comes in two types: Vernier caliper and digital caliper.
Vernier calipers have a main scale and a sliding vernier scale, while digital calipers provide direct readings on an LCD screen.

Read More
Micrometer

Micrometers are used to measure very small distances with high precision.
They consist of a calibrated screw mechanism and anvil to measure thickness, outer diameters, and depths.

Read More
Dial Indicator

Dial indicators, also known as dial gauges, are used to measure small linear distances and displacements.
They have a needle that points to a scale, providing readings with a higher resolution than Vernier calipers.

Read More
Surface Roughness Tester

This instrument measures the texture or roughness of a surface.
It is essential for quality control in manufacturing processes where surface finish plays a critical role.

Read More
Coordinate Measuring Machine (CMM)

CMM is a sophisticated device used to measure the dimensions of complex components with high precision.
It uses a probe to scan the surface of the object and records the data in a computer system.

Read More
Tachometer

A tachometer measures the rotational speed of a shaft or a rotating component.
It is commonly used to check the speed of engines, motors, and other rotating machinery.

Read More
Thermocouples and Thermometers

These instruments are used to measure temperature in mechanical systems.
Thermocouples generate a voltage proportional to the temperature difference, while thermometers use various principles to determine temperature.

Read More
Protractors and Angle Finders

Protractors and angle finders are used to measure angles in degrees.
They are particularly useful in applications involving precision machining or assembly.
