Power Generation Machines


Steam Turbine

Steam turbines are devices that convert the energy stored in high-pressure steam into mechanical work.
They are widely used in power plants and industrial processes to generate electricity and drive various machines.

Gas Turbine

Gas turbines, also known as combustion turbines, use natural gas or other gases as a fuel source. They work on the principle of Brayton cycle, where air is compressed, mixed with fuel, ignited, and then directed onto turbine blades to produce mechanical energy.
This mechanical energy is used to generate electricity through a connected generator.

Hydroelectric Turbines

Hydroelectric power plants use the potential energy of water stored in dams or rivers to generate electricity. Water is released from a reservoir, and its kinetic energy turns the turbine connected to a generator.
The flowing water then exits the turbine and continues downstream.

Wind Turbines

Wind turbines convert wind energy into electricity. As the wind blows, it causes the turbine blades to rotate, transferring the mechanical energy to the generator.
Wind power is a renewable energy source with minimal environmental impact.

Diesel Generators

Diesel generators use diesel engines to generate electricity.
They are often used in remote areas or as backup power sources when the primary power grid is unavailable.
Diesel generators can quickly provide power during emergencies.

Gasoline Generators

Gasoline generators work similarly to diesel generators, but they use gasoline as the fuel source.
They are commonly used for portable or temporary power needs.
