Types Of Guages

Mechanical gauges, also known as mechanical measuring instruments, are widely used in mechanical engineering for accurate measurement of physical quantities.
Here are some of the most common types of mechanical gauges, along with their detailed descriptions.


Types of Guages
  • CALLIPER
  • MICROMETER
  • DIAL INDICATOR
  • SURFACE ROUGHNESS TESTER
  • COORDINATE MEASURING MACHINE (CMM)
  • TACHOMETER
  • THERMOCOUPLES AND THERMOMETERS
  • PROTRACTORS AND ANGLE FINDERS
Vernier Calliper

A Vernier caliper is a precise measuring instrument used to measure the dimensions of an object. It consists of a main scale and a sliding Vernier scale. The main scale provides the whole number measurement, while the Vernier scale gives the fractional part.
By aligning the zero points of both scales, the measurement can be read with high accuracy.

Micrometer Screw Guage

The micrometer screw gauge is a highly accurate device used to measure very small distances, thickness, and outer diameters. It comprises a calibrated screw and a thimble.
The screw has a pitch (distance traveled per revolution), and the thimble is rotated to achieve the measurement.

Read More
Height Gauge (Height Caliper)

A height gauge is used to measure the height of objects or the depth of holes accurately.
It has a base with a vertical measuring scale and a sliding head that allows for precise measurements.

Read More
Depth Gauge

A depth gauge is used to measure the depth of holes, slots, or recesses in a workpiece.
It usually consists of a rod with a graduated scale and a measuring tip.

Read More
Feeler Gauge

A feeler gauge is a set of thin metal strips of varying thicknesses used to measure gaps or clearances between two parts.
It is particularly useful in settings where a specific gap needs to be maintained.

Read More
Thread Gauges

Thread gauges are used to measure the pitch diameter, pitch, and angle of screw threads.
Examples include thread plug gauges and thread ring gauges.

Read More
Bore Gauge

A bore gauge is employed to measure the diameter of holes or cylindrical bores accurately.
It consists of a measuring head, anvils, and a dial indicator.

Read More
Radius Guage

A radius gauge is a set of blades or pins with specific radii, used to measure or verify the radius of curved surfaces.

Read More
Bevel Protractor

A bevel protractor is used to measure and set angles, particularly in machining and fabrication tasks.
It consists of a protractor head with a graduated dial and an adjustable blade.

Read More
Angle Guage Block

An angle gauge block is a precision instrument used for measuring and verifying angles.
It often comes as a set of various angle blocks that can be combined to create different angles.

Read More
Dial Test Indicator (DTI)

A dial test indicator is similar to a dial indicator, but it is designed to measure lateral displacement or run-out in a machine or workpiece.
It is mounted on a flexible arm, making it versatile for various measurements.

Read More
Snap Gauge (Gap Gauge)

A snap gauge is a fixed limit gauge used for fast and straightforward pass-fail assessments.
It checks whether a workpiece's dimensions are within the acceptable tolerance range.
