Measuring Instruments

Measuring instruments have specific measurement ranges, indicating the minimum and maximum values they can accurately measure.
The resolution refers to the smallest increment that the instrument can detect or display, which affects its ability to capture fine details.

Various types of guages

Pressure gauges measure the pressure of fluids or gases.
They consist of a sensing element, such as a Bourdon tube or diaphragm,
which responds to pressure changes and translates them into readable measurements.
