Energy Conversion


1. Law of Conservation of Energy

The law of conservation of energy states that energy cannot be created or destroyed; it can only change from one form to another.
The total energy of an isolated system remains constant.
Mathematical Expression: ΔE=Q−W
ΔE = Change in total energy of the system Q = Heat added to the system W = Work done by the system

2. Mechanical Energy Conservation (Work-Energy Principle)

The work-energy principle states that the work done on an object is equal to the change in its mechanical energy.
Mathematical Expression: W=ΔKE+ΔPE
​ Where: W = Work done on the object ΔKE = Change in kinetic energy of the object ΔPE = Change in potential energy of the object

3. Kinetic Energy (KE) Formula

The kinetic energy of an object in motion is given by:
KE=1/2mv^2
Where: KE = Kinetic energy (Joules)
m = Mass of the object (kg)
v = Velocity of the object (m/s)

4. Potential Energy (PE) Formula

The potential energy of an object due to its position or height is given by:
PE=mgℎ Where: PE = Potential energy (Joules)
m = Mass of the object (kg)
g = Acceleration due to gravity (m/s²)
ℎ = Height above a reference level (m)

5. Thermal Energy Formula

Thermal energy is the internal energy of a system due to the motion of its particles.
For ideal gases, the thermal energy (U) is related to the temperature (T) and the number of moles (n) by: U=3/2nRT
Where:
U = Thermal energy (Joules)
R = Gas constant (8.314 J/(mol·K))

6. Electrical Power Formula

Electrical power is the rate at which electrical energy is consumed or produced in an electric circuit.
P=IV Where:
P = Electrical power (Watts)
I = Current in the circuit (Amperes)
V = Voltage across the circuit (Volts)
