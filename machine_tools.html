Machine Tools


Lathe Machine

A lathe machine is a versatile tool used for machining cylindrical, conical, and flat surfaces by rotating the workpiece while a cutting tool is applied to remove material.
Lathes are commonly used for turning operations to create shafts, pins, bolts, and other rotational parts.
They come in various types, including manual lathes and CNC (Computer Numerical Control) lathes, which offer automated precision and repeatability.

Milling Machine

Milling machines are used to remove material from a workpiece by rotating multi-point cutting tools.
They are capable of performing complex operations, such as cutting gears, slots, and contours.
Milling machines can be classified into horizontal and vertical types based on the spindle orientation.
CNC milling machines offer computerized control for precise and automated machining.

Drilling Machine

Drilling machines are designed to create holes in workpieces using drill bits.
They come in various types, including benchtop, upright, radial, and CNC drills.
Drilling machines are commonly used in metalworking, woodworking, and construction industries.

Grinding Machine

Grinding machines use abrasive wheels to remove material and produce fine finishes on workpieces.
They are used for precision grinding and can achieve tight tolerances and smooth surfaces.
Grinding machines are available in various types, such as surface grinders, cylindrical grinders, and tool and cutter grinders.

Shaping Machine

Shaping machines use reciprocating motion to remove material from a workpiece and create flat surfaces or other geometries.
The workpiece is fed against a cutting tool to achieve the desired shape.
Shaping machines are used in industries like automotive, aerospace, and general metalworking.

CNC Machine (Computer Numerical Control)

CNC machines are automated versions of traditional machine tools controlled by computers.
They use coded instructions to control the movements of the cutting tools, allowing for precise and complex machining operations.
CNC machines are used in various applications, such as milling, turning, and drilling.

Boring Machine

Boring machines are used to enlarge existing holes or create large-diameter holes in workpieces.
They use single-point cutting tools to remove material and are commonly used in manufacturing, construction, and mining.

Broaching Machine

Broaching machines use a broach, a multi-tooth cutting tool, to remove material in a controlled way to create holes, keyways, and other shapes in workpieces.
Broaching is often used for high-precision machining in small to medium-sized parts.

Planer Machine

Planer machines are used to produce flat surfaces on large and heavy workpieces.
They use a single-point cutting tool that moves along the workpiece's surface in a linear motion.
